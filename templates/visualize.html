Class Distribution

The bar chart shows the number of images across four classes: Gray Leaf Spot, Blight, Healthy, and Common Rust, highlighting a significant class imbalance, particularly the dominance of the "Healthy" class.

  • Healthy Class Dominates: The "Healthy" class has the most images (over 1000), creating a significant class imbalance that may lead to bias in predictions.
  • Underrepresented Classes: "Gray Leaf Spot" and "Common Rust" have the fewest images (each under 250), which may result in poor generalization and high misclassification rates for these classes.
  • Moderate Representation for Blight: The "Blight" class has a moderate number of images, making it less affected by imbalance compared to the other minority classes.

Image Dimension Distribution

The scatter plot shows the relationship between the width and height of images in the dataset. The points are concentrated at smaller dimensions, with a gradual spread towards larger dimensions, indicating a range of image sizes.

  • Concentration in Small Dimensions: Most images have dimensions below 1000x1000, suggesting the dataset predominantly contains smaller-sized images.
  • Proportional Scaling: The linear trend indicates that width and height are generally proportional, maintaining consistent aspect ratios.
  • Few Large Images: A small number of images have significantly larger dimensions, which might require resizing for consistency in preprocessing.

Aspect Ratio Distribution

The aspect ratio distribution shows that most images in the dataset are square (aspect ratio 1.0), with fewer images having wider or taller formats. This indicates a lack of diversity in image shapes, which could influence model performance if not addressed through preprocessing or augmentation.

  • Most Images are Square: The majority of images have an aspect ratio of 1.0, indicating a dominant presence of square-shaped images in the dataset.
  • Low Diversity in Aspect Ratios: A few images deviate from the square format, but non-square aspect ratios (e.g., 1.5, 2.0) are underrepresented.
  • Imbalance May Impact Model: The heavy skew towards square images could lead to bias in model training unless preprocessing or augmentation is applied.

Brightness Distribution

The brightness distribution illustrates the pixel intensity levels across all images, ranging from 40 to 200. The data appears to follow a roughly normal distribution, with a peak near 120, indicating that most images have mid-range brightness levels.

  • Balanced Brightness:The majority of images have brightness values concentrated around 120, suggesting a balanced exposure for most of the dataset.
  • Limited ExtremesFew images have very low (dark) or very high (bright) brightness, indicating minimal presence of overexposed or underexposed images.
  • Uniformity Opportunity:The overall distribution suggests consistent lighting across the dataset, which can reduce the need for aggressive brightness normalization during preprocessing.
